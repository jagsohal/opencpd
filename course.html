COURSE OVERVIEW

WHY JOIN THIS COURSE?

Young people make extensive use of social media.


This level of engagement is inevitably impacting on their learning, and their health and wellbeing. If, as teachers, we want to reach young people and understand something of thier worlds and offer support, we need to know how they engage with social media, what they learn from it and how that may influence their health and wellbeing. While many influences may be positive, there are also likely to be periods of vulnerability in young people’s lives where the sheer scale, intensity and pervasiveness of social media could act to intensify those vulnerabilities. 

THE COURSE

This online professional development course is designed to support primary, secondary and pre-service teachers from a range of subject areas – as well as support staff - better understand how to use the power of digital technologies in positive and informed ways to support, develop and improve young people’s earning, and their health and wellbeing.


By the end of the course you will:


1. Have an enhanced understanding of the complex and dynmaic ways in which young people navigate social media, and the ways in which social media shapes learning and health and wellbeing 

2. Develop the ability to evaluate, design and use digital resources with your students

3. Have an enhanced level of confience to engage with social media in delivering education, and in support young people’s health and wellbeing 

LEARN WITH EXPERTS

The course has been developed by the School of Sport, Exercise and Rehabilitation Sciences at the University of Birmingham, UK.


It is led by Dr Victoria Goodyear, who has extensive experience of supporting teachers, through online CPD, video blogging and social media.

COURSE CONTENT AND STRUCTURE

The course is online and free, and will take place over 12 months and cover 3 main topics


1. Understanding how young people use and navigate social media for thier learning, and health and wellbeing - you will learn from young people about how they use social media, and be supported to enage with your students about their social media engagemnt 

2. Designing a social media curriculum/pedagogical practices - you will be supported to design and then test different approaches using social media to help your students learn 

3. Evaluation - we will provide you with new tools to evaluate your practice and student learning, in a social media context

4. Best Practice - you will be invited to present in our online conference and enter a competion to publish your curriculum/pedagogical approach in our forthcoming book. 

WHO IS THE COURSE FOR?

Pastoral Care: Educators confidence and ability to be attentive CYP’s digital needs will be improved, maximising the support they receive from tutors

Computer Science/ICT Education: Educators capacity to design curricula that reflects CYP’s complex and dynamic uses of contemporary digital technologies and media will be strengthened

Personal Social and Health Education/Citizenship: Educators capacity to develop young people’s digital literacy skills in this curriculum area will be improved (this is a key priority area for education in the UK within this subject field)

Physical and Health Education: Educators capabilities to use digital technologies in relevant ways to support health and wellbeing education will be strengthened.
